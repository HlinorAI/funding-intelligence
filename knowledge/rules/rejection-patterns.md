# Rejection patterns

- Generic deck without a project-specific thesis.
- “We support this chain too” without native architecture, distribution or KPI.
- Asking for payroll/runway from a public-good or retro mechanism.
- No live demo, deployed contracts, repository or measurable usage where shipping is a hard gate.
- Milestones that depend on unnamed future partners.
- Vanity followers, impressions or testnet activity presented as product traction.
- Inflated budget with no cost-to-outcome logic.
- Proprietary product disguised as open source only to qualify for a grant.
- Confusing a closed season, stale landing page or ecosystem treasury with an open intake.
- Treating investment, liquidity incentives, audit subsidy or accelerator access as non-dilutive cash.
- No legal/KYC/security readiness for institutional or diligence-heavy routes.
- No owner for adoption and maintenance after the funded milestone.
