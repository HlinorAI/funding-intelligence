# AI fit rules

## Route taxonomy

1. `startup_program` — community, technical resources, and founder support.
2. `cloud_credits` — compute/API credits that offset build cost; not funding.
3. `enterprise_bd` — marketplace, co-sell, design partners, and procurement access.
4. `accelerator` — cohort, mentorship, investor access, and possible investment.
5. `research_grant` — public research or deeptech milestone funding; not represented in v1.
6. `open_source_sponsorship` — support for a reusable public artifact; not represented in v1.

## AI-specific gates

- An AI label alone is not enough: identify the product, customer, technical layer, and proof.
- For agent infrastructure, separate model/API usage from proprietary technical differentiation.
- For B2B/enterprise routing, a customer workflow, pilot, or named buyer matters more than generic AI positioning.
- For cloud credits, verify company eligibility, current funding stage, account/domain, and provider requirements.
- For accelerator/investment, require team, product, market, and a concise application thesis.
- For research/deeptech, require technical novelty, reproducible scope, and a research artifact.
- Never call cloud credits a grant, and never call enterprise access an investment.

## Prototype-stage routing implication

With a prototype, repository, and unknown users/revenue/pilots, the first routes are:

- validate the technical demo and customer workflow;
- verify startup and cloud-support eligibility;
- seek enterprise discovery or pilot conversations;
- defer accelerator/investment until product and market proof are clearer.
